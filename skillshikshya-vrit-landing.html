Why This Exists

Fix It Ourselves.

You deserve to learn from people who actually build. Vrit Technologies has spent years working with national and multinational clients — shipping real software, solving real problems, building real engineers. We know exactly what separates a candidate who gets hired from one who doesn't.

The gap isn't intelligence. It isn't effort. It's exposure. Most graduates never touch a production codebase. Never join a standup. Never feel the weight of a deadline that matters to a real user.

SkillShikshya exists to close that gap — not with simulation, but with reality. Your mentors are the engineers who built Chairlyo, integrated WhatsApp APIs, and debugged live systems for clients. They teach what they do — because they're still doing it.

The Weekly Ritual

Every
Friday
Hits Different.

Technical skills get you an interview. Everything else gets you the job. Friday is where we build that everything else.

Knowledge Quiz CV Workshop HR Simulation Futsal Ethics Interview Prep Team Bonding
01The Knowledge Quiz
›
Run every Friday without exception. Not to punish — to expose gaps before an interviewer does. The quiz is your early warning system. The students who resist it most are the ones who need it most.
02CV & Portfolio Sessions
›
We go line by line. A CV that tells the truth about real work you actually did — because you did it here. That's what opens doors. Generic templates get filtered out before a human ever sees them.
03HR Simulation Rounds
›
Behavioral rounds. Salary negotiation. Culture-fit interviews. We run real mock interviews so the actual interview feels familiar, not frightening. Walk in prepared. Walk out with the offer.
04Futsal & The Pitch
›
The team that knows how to lose a game together, laugh, and come back — that's the team that ships under pressure. Sportsmanship is a professional skill. We train it like one.
05Ethics & Professional Culture
›
How you handle a mistake. How you give feedback. How you disagree without breaking a relationship. The soft stuff is the hard stuff. We don't ignore it — we make it a discipline.
